# Production decisions

The design explicitly leaves these choices open. The initial release uses conservative, reversible defaults:

| Decision | Release default | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Currency | Internal audited accounts; no Vault dependency | Keeps treasury transactions authoritative |
| Worker navigation | Waypoint navigator behind `Navigator` | Avoids coupling task ownership to a hidden mob |
| Perishable goods | Disabled | Prevents early micromanagement; batch model remains available |
| Campaign timing | 24-hour preparation, 14-day maximum | Matches Chapter 4 configuration baseline |
| Offline damage | 10% structural multiplier | Sabotage remains possible without offline wiping |
| Private insurance | Disabled | Automatic repair remains limited to registered public structures |
| Vanilla automation | Allowed within one territory owner; blocked across ownership/wilderness boundaries | Keeps normal settlement machinery usable while closing actorless claim bypasses |
| Folia | Architecture-compatible schedulers; Paper is the certified target | Avoids an unsupported compatibility claim |
| Paper dependency | `26.2.build.60-beta` | Latest official 26.2 beta at project bootstrap (2026-07-12) |

## Post-1.0 remediation decisions

| Sprint | Decision | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Money commands use integer cents, matching every persisted `*_minor` field. | Avoids floating-point ambiguity and keeps commands identical to audited ledger values. |
| 1 | Player wallets start at zero; repeatable daily Harbor jobs are the non-admin currency entry point. | Currency enters through visible gameplay and a bounded daily source instead of unexplained login grants. |
| 1 | Frontier Harbor has a 250,000-cent daily starter-job budget, low guaranteed buy prices and high limited sell prices. | It guarantees liquidity without outcompeting player settlements; its daily reset is an explicit controlled source/sink. |
| 1 | Player settlements receive 10,000 cents, 64 wheat and 16 bread once at founding. | This is a finite bootstrap buffer: it prevents immediate collapse but still requires Harbor work, taxes, production and trade. |
| Release | Published 1.0.0 remains immutable; remediation checkpoints are development builds and the final audited artifact receives a new release-candidate version. | Replacing a public artifact under the same version would break reproducibility and checksum trust. |
| 2 | Claim authorization is served from one atomically replaced PostgreSQL projection and never queries the database on a Paper event thread. | Interaction events must be decided synchronously without blocking a region/main thread. |
| 2 | Citizens may build, break, use containers/interactions/entities/buckets/crops/vehicles; recruits may only interact; architects, builder masters and mayors also control automation, hanging entities, fire and redstone. | This gives residents usable claims while reserving high-impact automation controls for trusted roles. Explicit `PROTECTION_*` overrides take precedence. |
| 2 | Active campaign attackers may only pass the generic protection policy for block breaking; the existing breach service still journals and authorizes the actual mutation. | Campaign status must not become a general container, placement or redstone bypass. |
| 2 | Automatic inventory, fire and redstone propagation may not cross claim-owner boundaries. | These are the actorless exploit paths; same-settlement automation remains available. |
| 3 | Structural damage uses a two-phase database/world protocol: breach capacity and an `AUTHORIZED` journal are committed first, then the region-thread mutation is confirmed as `APPLIED`. | PostgreSQL and Minecraft chunks cannot share one transaction; an explicit recoverable intermediate state closes the phantom-journal crash gap. |
| 3 | A repaired coordinate reuses its durable journal identity but increments `generation` and creates a new breach spend. | The positional uniqueness invariant still prevents duplicate rows, while a legitimate re-break is no longer free or invisible. |
| 3 | Prepared material consumption is retained across worker/entity loss and can be rebound; release reuses the same per-task consumption row. | Material is only consumed on confirmed placement, so restart/disconnect cannot duplicate or lose it. |
| 3 | Five failed task attempts move work to `REVIEW_REQUIRED`; completed orders archive after 24 hours. | Infinite hot-loop retries are unsafe, while a delayed archive preserves an operational inspection window. |
| 4 | Building registration is only successful after a bounded live-world survey passes the type validator and the database repeats authorization, claim, overlap and district checks in the write transaction. | World state cannot be locked together with PostgreSQL; bounded scanning plus transactional revalidation prevents stale registration decisions and oversized scans. |
| 4 | Registration records `PLANNED`, `UNDER_CONSTRUCTION`, `VALIDATING` and `ACTIVE` as durable history in one transaction; damage later moves active buildings through `DAMAGED`, `DISABLED` and `DESTROYED`. | The full lifecycle remains auditable without exposing partially committed buildings to gameplay. |
| 4 | District compatibility accepts an optional district type until Sprint 5 supplies persistent district identities and boundaries. | This preserves strict type rules now while allowing Sprint 5 to replace the temporary type hint with authoritative district records. |
| 5 | Districts are non-overlapping, remain wholly inside controlled settlement claims and are referenced by UUID from buildings. | A stable identity prevents rename/type strings from silently reassigning buildings; spatial ownership is rechecked in every mutation transaction. |
| 5 | District budgets are audited allocations capped by the live settlement treasury, not separate money accounts. | This prevents double-counted currency while still allowing priorities and reports to reserve political spending intent. |
| 5 | District history deliberately survives district deletion; operational worker/storage/budget rows cascade and building references are cleared. | Administrative history must remain auditable, while deleted districts must not leave active gameplay ownership behind. |
| 5 | District bonuses are capped at 20% and applied through the database-backed district projection to production, housing growth, maintenance, defense cost, trade queue priority, worker efficiency and repair priority. | Small bounded modifiers make specialization meaningful without allowing stacked multiplicative runaway; one building belongs to at most one district. |
| 6 | Founding uses a database fee reservation plus compensating material refund because a Minecraft inventory and PostgreSQL cannot share one atomic transaction. | The explicit `RESERVED`, `COMPLETED`, and `REFUNDED` saga closes fee/material loss on validation or database failure and is recoverable after timeout. |
| 6 | The default minimum is one accepted founder, while the schema and validation retain a configurable minimum-founder invariant. | A fresh or low-population server must remain playable without admin intervention; multiplayer servers can raise the invariant without a schema change. |
| 6 | A core requires solid non-ocean terrain inside the border and 128 blocks of separation; founding consumes a bell which becomes the physical core. | The checks prevent overlapping capitals and invalid/off-world settlements while giving the lifecycle a visible in-world anchor. |
| 6 | Abandoned/disbanded settlements become ruins for 30 days; claims are archived and only still-wilderness claims can be restored. | Recovery is possible without stealing territory acquired by another settlement during abandonment. |
| 6 | Merge requires both mayors, no active campaign, and no kingdom membership; the target keeps its identity and active warehouse while source free stock and capacity transfer. | This avoids ambiguous diplomacy/war ownership and respects the one-active-warehouse invariant while preserving reserved stock references. |
| 7 | Physical edge scans are capped at 256 blocks and require 85% connected recognized surface, two-block minimum width, quality 40 and maximum per-sample slope 1.5. | The cap bounds region-thread work; the thresholds tolerate decorative intersections without accepting teleport links or unusable paths. |
| 7 | Edge health and capacity are derived from the physical survey, while importance is a mayor/architect planning value from 0-100 and traffic is incremented by actual routed shipments. | Players cannot claim arbitrary throughput; operational and political values remain separate and auditable. |
| 7 | Paper is the certified physical-survey target; the scheduler boundary is retained but cross-region Folia route scanning is not claimed. | A multi-chunk synchronous read is valid on certified Paper but would need per-region aggregation before claiming Folia support. |
| 8 | Caravan cargo exists only in database shipment reservations/items; chest-llama entities are disposable presentation projections. | Entity death, unload, restart, plugin disable and chunk transitions cannot create, destroy or duplicate authoritative cargo. |
| 8 | Caravans materialize within 96 blocks of a player and hold only their current chunk ticket; unobserved travel advances in PostgreSQL once per second. | This provides visible travel without permanently loading routes or making simulation depend on player proximity. |
| 8 | Combat pauses the shipment clock; escorts reduce persisted damage by 25%, and health at or below 25 triggers a bounded reverse retreat before rerouting with 50 health. | Combat has logistical consequences without creating an unrecoverable low-health reroute loop. |
| 9 | Population changes once per UTC day from persisted housing, food, safety and prosperity factors; migration and natural change are recorded separately. | A deterministic daily cadence avoids tick-driven population explosions and makes reports explain why a settlement changed. |
| 9 | One active residential building supplies ten capacity plus a five-person core allowance; district housing bonuses add bounded flat capacity. | Capacity remains legible to players and tied to validated buildings without inventing individual household entities. |
| 9 | Worker efficiency is capped at 150 and combines 50% skill, 30% morale, 20% experience score and the district bonus. | Experience and morale matter without overwhelming profession skill or enabling unbounded veteran output. |
| 9 | Worker age advances one day per demographic cycle and retirement changes the authoritative worker state, removing the worker from production selection. | Retirement is durable gameplay rather than a cosmetic report field. |
| 10 | Companies have dedicated audited accounts funded from player wallets; they are associated with a settlement but do not reuse its treasury. | This separates private capital from government money and makes invoices, loans and taxes traceable. |
| 10 | Company loans are funded by the associated settlement treasury and accrue simple daily interest from annual basis points; interest is paid before principal. | The model is deterministic, integer-only and avoids compounding more frequently than the simulation cadence. |
| 10 | Business tax is assessed daily on company cash balance and is collected only when funds are available; one assessment exists per company/date. | Tax retries cannot double-charge, and underfunded businesses remain visibly due instead of going negative. |
| 10 | Procurement pays the posted maximum price on delivered warehouse stock; emergency buying costs twice the local 30-day median or twice a conservative fallback and pays Frontier Harbor. | Procurement creates player supply demand, while emergency supply remains a deliberately bad, auditable last resort. |
| 10 | Trade and price history remain derived from immutable trades/price observations; commercial events add a separate unified report rather than duplicating market truth. | One authoritative source per economic fact prevents divergent histories. |
| 11 | A campaign result is accepted only from `RESOLUTION`, is unique per campaign and completes campaign state in the same database transaction as its consequences. | Retried commands or restart recovery cannot apply territory or money effects twice. |
| 11 | Conquest and annexation transfer the full losing settlement; territory concession transfers only claims selected by completed campaign objectives and assets spatially attached to those claims. | Objective scope gives limited wars bounded consequences while decisive outcomes remain complete. |
| 11 | Occupation preserves settlement identity, liberation removes occupation, and annexation also disables the source settlement simulation. | Political control and permanent absorption remain distinct, reversible concepts. |
| 11 | Tribute is a persisted recurring obligation with one unique ledger reference per due date; reparations are a single audited transfer capped by available treasury funds. | Scheduled retries remain idempotent and no outcome can create a negative treasury balance. |
| 11 | Civil-war and kingdom-intervention results are durable campaign facts in Sprint 11; kingdom membership, secession and council consequences are applied by the Sprint 12 kingdom integration layer. | Campaign resolution can ship independently without inventing partial kingdom rules before that transactional boundary exists. |
| 12 | Every member settlement has one vote and its mayor casts or replaces that ballot; passage requires a strict majority of current member settlements. | Kingdom politics represent member governments rather than online-player count and cannot be captured by alts. |
| 12 | KING and COUNCIL govern policy/finance, MARSHAL approves war, and DIPLOMAT handles treaties; only the KING may transfer kingdom roles. | Separated authority limits treasury and war abuse while retaining a clear succession path. |
| 12 | A kingdom campaign requires a persisted, unexpired approval consumed by exactly one declaration; active non-aggression and alliance treaties prevent approval. | Commands cannot bypass kingdom politics and approval replay cannot launch multiple wars. |
| 12 | Kingdom tax is a daily, idempotent percentage of each member treasury, capped at 25%; underfunded members never become negative. | Shared projects receive predictable funding without creating currency or making settlements insolvent through debt. |
| 12 | `PEACEFUL_SECESSION=ALLOW` immediately releases a member; otherwise secession creates a contested authorization and only an independence campaign outcome removes membership. | Civil war has an explicit opt-in and a recoverable transactional path instead of deleting membership before conflict is resolved. |
| 13 | `/frontier` is the primary Paper Dialog entry point; every screen action calls the same application-service-backed command path retained for accessibility and console fallback. | Dialog and command behavior cannot drift into two gameplay implementations, and commands/listeners still contain no transaction logic. |
| 13 | Dialogs close after any submitted action, while contextual claim/upgrade callbacks additionally use player-bound, aggregate-bound, expiring single-use tokens. | Closing prevents accidental double-click submission; tokens protect the highest-risk callbacks against replay even if a client resends them. |
| 13 | One tested static screen catalog owns labels, navigation and command templates; the Paper adapter only renders it and supplies typed input controls. | Navigation coverage can be regression-tested without booting a Minecraft client and obsolete command targets become visible in review. |
| 14 | Admin viewers are read-only, use a fixed allowlist of parameterized SQL queries and execute asynchronously; no viewer exposes arbitrary table or SQL input. | Operational access cannot mutate gameplay, block the Paper thread or become a SQL-injection surface. |
| 14 | Heatmaps are capped at radius 12 and render persisted chunk claims rather than scanning live chunks. | Output and query work stay bounded while ownership remains consistent with the authoritative influence state. |
| 14 | Live diagnostics combine in-process Micrometer counters with database-derived operational counts at request time. | Runtime failures and authoritative backlog are both visible without maintaining a second persistent metrics truth. |
| 15 | Regional weather is derived deterministically from region, UTC date and season, then persisted once per day with severity. | Restarts and multiple supervisors observe the same weather without an external API or random replay divergence. |
| 15 | Infrastructure decays at most daily; overdue maintenance, winter, storm severity, bridge type and traffic add small integer damage recorded per edge. | Physical infrastructure matters without the prior 30-minute cadence destroying roads faster than players can react. |
| 15 | World-event start effects use a unique event/city/impact row before mutating authoritative state. | A retry or crash cannot apply plague deaths, flood damage, migration or stock loss twice. |
| 15 | Event selection is a pure priority policy: infrastructure disaster, flood, plague, bandits, harvest failure, trade fair, migration wave, then harvest festival. | Severe safety conditions take precedence over positive festivals and every trigger can be unit-tested without clock or database coupling. |
| 15 | Structural-damage registration takes a PostgreSQL transaction advisory lock keyed by campaign/world/block coordinate before reading or inserting the journal. | Two simultaneous hits can no longer both observe an empty coordinate and collide on the legacy uniqueness invariant. |
| 16 | Dynamic events are derived from persisted gameplay sources and share the existing world-event lifecycle, objective and reward tables. | A single event truth avoids parallel quest state and makes detection restart-safe. |
| 16 | One source may create at most one non-archived event per 24 hours; detection is capped per cycle and uses fixed parameter-free source queries. | Frequent supervisors cannot spam players or turn growing tables into unbounded work. |
| 16 | Event participation is settlement-citizen gated, contributions accumulate transactionally and completion is driven by the objective target. | Events remain multiplayer gameplay while contribution retries cannot erase prior progress. |
| 16 | Escort-event join writes the caravan's authoritative escort in the same transaction as participation. | The event has real simulation effect and cannot disagree with caravan combat mitigation. |
| 17 | Official research, wonders and mega projects must exist in immutable database-backed catalogs and match their declared era, material and requirement. | Free-text endgame content cannot bypass balance, prerequisites or uniqueness rules. |
| 17 | Completed content creates one durable kingdom unlock carrying a JSON effect; gameplay services read only the effects they own. | Effects remain auditable and extensible without coupling every module to a central mutable bonus object. |
| 17 | Rankings use a deterministic score of prestige + population + era tier + completed-content weights and never persist rank position. | Rankings cannot become stale; ties are stable by kingdom name and raw components remain visible. |
| 17 | Unique wonder keys remain globally unique, while research and mega-project completion are kingdom-scoped. | Wonders are true world firsts; technology and national infrastructure remain attainable by every kingdom. |
| 18 | Every player Frontier command, including `/frontier` menu opens and operator commands, uses the same per-player rate limiter. | UI or elevated permission must not become a command/packet amplification bypass. |
| 18 | PostgreSQL deadlock and serialization aborts retry the complete transaction at most three times; other SQL errors fail immediately. | Retrying only rollback-safe database work improves concurrency without hiding constraints or looping indefinitely. |
| 18 | Dynamic event sources use both an advisory transaction lock and an open-state unique index. | The lock avoids expected conflicts; the database invariant remains the final defense across multiple server processes. |
| 18 | Security status is evaluated from live invariants and indexes and never auto-repairs findings. | An audit must not silently mutate evidence or guess how to resolve an incident. |
| 19 | Scheduler profiling records queue delay and execution separately and tags periodic database work by subsystem; region/global callbacks are timed independently. | Slow SQL must not be mistaken for tick work, and startup spikes must remain distinguishable from steady-state cycles. |
| 19 | Performance diagnostics are cumulative, lock-free counters plus PostgreSQL statistics queried on demand. | Profiling adds negligible hot-path overhead and does not create a metrics-write workload. |
| 19 | V31 indexes match actual due-queue and ownership lookup predicates; batches remain bounded by existing supervisor configuration. | Indexes reduce selection work while caps prevent one cycle from monopolizing database or scheduler capacity. |
| 19 | The JFR idle baseline is an engineering regression gate, not a multiplayer capacity claim. | Player-scale conclusions require the synthetic load matrix in Sprint 20. |
| 20 | Scale tiers use relationally complete synthetic players and real gateway calls, not mocked service returns. | Database contention, mapping cost and cross-feature query load must be represented for useful results. |
| 20 | Acceptance is p95 under 1,000 ms, workload under 60 seconds, clean financial/stock/idempotency invariants and a successful Paper security/performance gate. | Latency alone is insufficient if load corrupts state or blocks Paper threads. |
| 20 | The published 500-player result is explicitly a server/database workload claim, not a network, chunk-generation or client-rendering claim. | Authentic capacity reporting must state what was and was not simulated. |
| 21 | Sounds, particles, titles and broadcasts live in `PaperPresentationService` and are called only after authoritative service results or lifecycle transitions. | Presentation failure or removal cannot change money, territory, repairs, war or settlement truth. |
| 21 | Repair particles/sounds run on the block's region task and campaign/settlement announcements run on global/entity-safe callbacks. | Polish must respect Paper scheduler ownership and never introduce cross-thread Bukkit access. |
| 21 | Dialog mutation actions are visually yellow with explicit protected-action tooltips; read/navigation actions are aqua and reports use stable multi-line labels. | Players who cannot code still need to distinguish consequential actions and understand report output at a glance. |
| 22 | `docs/README.md` is the maintained manual index; migrations remain authoritative for exact SQL and the static Dialog catalog remains authoritative for screen actions. | A single navigable manual prevents duplicated guides from drifting while preserving executable sources of truth for details that change mechanically. |
| 23 | Final QA runs the complete integration journey on a newly created database and a newly generated Paper world without using Frontier admin commands to seed gameplay. | The gate exercises production migrations and public application services while keeping recovery/inspection privileges out of the player's progression path. |
| 24 | Critical numeric configuration is validated and fails startup when non-positive or internally impossible; database/async pools are capped at 64. | A release candidate must reject typo-driven hot loops, empty database identity and resource settings that can destabilize Paper instead of silently accepting them. |
| Master 1 | Feature status is graded `complete`, `partial`, `backend-only`, `placeholder`, `missing` or `blocked` from end-to-end evidence; a table/class/command by itself is never completion. | The previous RC status was truthful for its older roadmap but over-broad for the new Atlas, crime, web and history expansion scope. |
| Master 1 | Build time is the current Git commit timestamp and build revision is the packaged source HEAD. | Deterministic source metadata keeps repeated local builds reproducible; CI builds after a sprint commit identify that exact committed source. |
| Master 1 | Release-train versions must advance from existing `1.1.0-RC1`; lower alpha/beta names in the greenfield guideline will not be used. | Semantic versions must never move backwards. Stable `1.1.0` remains greater than every new prerelease identifier. |
| Master 2 | Runtime reload validates and classifies disk changes but never swaps captured service/supervisor settings in place; it reports the required restart. | A half-live reload can leave authorization, batch bounds and schedulers disagreeing, which is less safe than an explicit restart boundary. |
| Master 2 | Future waypoints, cartography, map-wall and web configs exist as typed, validated, disabled controls with no speculative gameplay keys. | This satisfies stable file layout without shipping unused switches or implying that missing modules are implemented. |
| Master 3 | Harbor may trade only bread, wheat, oak logs, cobblestone and iron ingots; config may remove but cannot add commodities to that starter allowlist. | A server typo must not turn the guaranteed NPC market into a diamond/netherite source or a late-game price floor. |
| Master 3 | The daily source is capped at the configured daily budget, each player's configured starter-job total is capped, and overlapping buy/sell offers must retain a loss-making spread. | These invariants keep Harbor a bounded launch aid instead of an inflation or arbitrage engine. |
| Master 4 | `TerritoryActionPolicy` is the single authorization vocabulary for Paper claim events; listeners only translate events and read the atomic projection. | Synchronous Paper events cannot block on PostgreSQL, and role/campaign behavior cannot drift between listeners. |
| Master 4 | Treaties and incidents are explicit policy context but never grant implicit property access; only explicit protection overrides can extend action rights. | Diplomatic friendliness must not silently become container, build or redstone permission. |
| Master 4 | Actorless movement and propagation are allowed only when source and target resolve to the same territory owner, including wilderness-to-wilderness. | Pistons, liquids, hoppers, fire, block explosions and redstone cannot be used to bypass a claim boundary. |
| Master 4 | Campaign attackers may pass only break/explosion policy, after which the objective/breach/journal service remains authoritative for mutation. | An active war cannot become a general interaction bypass and every structural mutation remains recoverable and auditable. |
| Master 5 | A positional damage journal keeps a stable UUID but every occurrence has a monotonically increasing generation linked uniquely to its breach spend. | Position lookup remains race-safe while re-break accounting is independently auditable and rejecting generation N cannot refund N-1. |
| Master 5 | World/chunk absence and temporary hostility defer a prepared task without consuming material, adding an attempt or granting worker experience. | Environmental availability is not repair failure; retained preparation makes restart/resume safe without retry exhaustion. |
| Master 5 | Repair purchase replay is serialized by idempotency key before eligible damage is locked; multi-task partial progress remains `REPAIRING`. | Concurrent commands cannot double-charge/order the same damage and a valid remaining task never becomes invisible to the lease queue. |
| Master 5 | Completion age starts at durable `completed_at`; only confirmed placement earns worker experience. | Long-running orders receive the configured inspection window and aborted/conflicting work cannot manufacture progression. |
| Release A | The first five-sprint checkpoint is `1.1.0-RC2`, not the roadmap's greenfield `1.1.0-alpha.1`. | RC1 already exists publicly; advancing the RC sequence preserves version ordering while stable `1.1.0` remains the final target. |
